## OUR CHARACTER: THE THREE VOICES

### Voice 1 — THE GUIDE
Knowledgeable without being superior. Leads through invitation, not instruction.
```
✓ "Here's how this works — let's explore it together"
✗ "You need to do X" or "You must..."
```

### Voice 2 — THE WITNESS
Observational, specific, honest. Calls out what's real without editorial judgment.
```
✓ "The cenotes of Yucatán sit where the asteroid hit — 65 million years of silence."
✗ "Experience the amazing and breathtaking cenotes!" (generic tourism copy)
```

### Voice 3 — THE CRAFTSPERSON
Technically precise where precision matters. Never vague about implementation.
```
✓ "The atmosphere shader renders at half resolution on mobile to protect battery."
✗ "We use advanced technology to optimize your experience."
```

## TONE CALIBRATION

### Awareness copy (social, ads, discovery)
```
Tone: Evocative, poetic, invitation without pressure
Length: Short. One idea per line.
Example:
  "The jungle doesn't wait for you to be ready."
  "Some places teach you who you are."
```

### Informational copy (onboarding, documentation, help text)
```
Tone: Clear, warm, specific
Length: As short as possible. Break into steps.
Example:
  "Drop a task file in tasks/queue/ — HERMES picks it up within 60 seconds."
```

### Transactional copy (CTAs, confirmations, receipts)
```
Tone: Action-oriented, confident, specific
No generic verbs: Submit, Save, OK, Continue
Example:
  ✓ "Book This Journey"
  ✓ "Send My Brief"
  ✓ "Save to My Querencia"
  ✗ "Submit" / "Save" / "Proceed"
```

## TYPOGRAPHY VOICE

**Display / Headlines:** Playfair Display — literary, editorial, earned authority
**Body text:** Lato — clean, accessible, does not compete with display
**Labels / UI elements:** DM Mono — precision, technical credibility without coldness

**Typography principle:** Headlines should feel like something you'd find carved in stone
or printed in a 1940s travel journal. Not a SaaS startup.

## THINGS WE DO NOT SAY

```
✗ "Cutting-edge AI" — vague tech-bro claim
✗ "Seamless experience" — filler
✗ "Best-in-class" — meaningless
✗ "Leverage" (as a verb) — corporate
✗ "Synergy" — never
✗ "Disrupt" — 2014 called
✗ "Unlock your potential" — self-help cliché
✗ "We're on a mission to..." — everyone says this
✗ Exclamation points in professional contexts — cheapens authority
✗ Emoji in formal documentation — Bambu texts are the exception
```

## KUPURI'S RELATIONSHIP WITH LATIN AMERICA

Not tourism. Not exoticism. Not "untouched paradise."

**What we say:**
- Specific places, specific stories, specific peoples
- The history that made a place what it is
- The tension between preservation and opportunity

**What we never do:**
- Generic stock photography of smiling locals
- "Off the beaten path" (tired phrase)
- Speak FOR communities — speak ABOUT them with respect
- Flatten Indigenous cultures into "vibes" or aesthetics
